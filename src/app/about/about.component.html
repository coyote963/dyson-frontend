What is Dyson City?

It's a website for collecting and displaying important statistics for the game Boring Man

DC collects that data from 4 servers: Spasman's Survival, Deathmatch, Capture the Flag, and Team Deathmatch

Once player data is captured, it is stored in an open database. To learn more about the specifications of the database API, click here

This application builds on top of this API and uses it to create a unique player ranking system for the 3 gamemodes

What is the ranking system?

The ranking system is built in order to predict the outcome of matches with two numbers: mu and sigma

You can think of every player's performance as a bell curve. Some days you play exceptionally well and some days exceptionally bad. But on most days you play just average

The mu and sigma are used to emulate this aspect of player performance

As you might know, rating systems are designed to favor least surprise when encountering a new result.

This means that when the system assumes a bell curve for you that is too narrow (thinking that your performance is very consistent), and you have a few big upsets, the system will be "surprised" enough that it widens your bell curve in order to account for this new surprise

In addition, if you play worse than your average a couple times, then it will move the bell curve so that your average is less.

What I just described is the purpose of the two parameters: Mu and Sigma. Mu governs the average performance of the player and Sigma is the consistency of the player.

It is customary to start the rating system with an average Mu and setting the Sigma to a large amount. In Dyson City's case, Mu is set to 25 and Sigma is set to 25/3

The system knows that it requires enough games/rounds to converge to a correct value. This is why for the "official" rating, it takes Mu - 3 * Sigma to account for this. In other words, there is a reward for shrinking the Sigma

It gets a lot more complicated when there is teams playing, and even more complicated when the teams don't have the same size. Luckily this is handled for with the rating system.

For Team Deathmatch, there is another rating for those who care mostly about Individual performance, with Elo.
